A positive integer n is said to be "prime-saturated" if the product of all the different positive prime factors of n is less than the square root of n. What is the greatest two-digit prime-saturated integer?
A.99
B.98
C.97
D.96
E.95

A. Distinct Prime Factors of 99: 3,11
Product = 3*11 = 33 > √99


B. Distinct Prime Factors of 98: 2,7
Product = 2*7 = 14 > √98

C. Distinct Prime factors of 97: 97

Product = 97 > √97

D. Distinct Prime factors of 96: 2,3
Product = 2*3 = 6 < √96

E. Distinct Prime Factors of 95: 5,19
Product = 5*19 = 95 > √95

所以正确答案选D
